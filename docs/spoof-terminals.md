# Spoof Terminals

Spoof terminals add one tactical choice without changing CyberStorm's core input model.

## What They Do

- A spoof terminal is a single-use map tile that appears in live sectors.
- Stepping onto it immediately burns the tile out and starts a short spoof window.
- During that window, hunters still take exactly one response step per player action, but they target the exit lane instead of the runner.

## Why It Is Fun

- It creates a real tradeoff every few turns instead of a passive bonus.
- Triggering a terminal can buy breathing room when hunters are collapsing on the runner.
- The same choice can also make the exit lane more dangerous later, because the redirected pack tends to bunch up near the gate.
- The mechanic fits the breach fantasy: the runner is not casting a spell, they are poisoning system routing for a few cycles.

## Readability Rules

- The tile itself is visually distinct on the map.
- The HUD shows a `SPOOF` timer while the route is live.
- The active route is drawn on the map so the player can see what pressure is being redirected.
- The effect is short and deterministic, so first-time players can learn it quickly.
